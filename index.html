Mahmoud Koko

My timeline

2022 - 2025:

I joined the group of Dr. Hilary C. Martin at Wellcome Sanger Institute in 2022 as a postdoctoral fellow. My work in the Martin Lab (Medical and Population Genomics) explores the genetics of developmental, behavioural and cognitive traits. I have worked on several topics:

  • Understanding the relationship between rare coding variants and sex differences in autism prevalence.
  • Curating exome sequencing data from UK Birth Cohorts and leveraging these data to understrand the genetics of cognitive traits in the population.
  • Using UK Biobank 500k genomes to investigate the association of non-coding rare variant burden and cognitive traits.

2016 - 2022:

Previously, I was a doctoral student at the Hertie Insisute for Clinical Brain Research. I studied the role of rare genetic variants in predisposing to common and rare epilepsy syndromes. These are the main areas I worked on:

  • Identifying rare variants predisposing to epilepsy in consanguineous Sudanese families.
  • Testing the differences in gene set enrichment between generalized and focal epilepsies.
  • Predicting and functionally validating the electrophysiological consequences of rare coding variants in ion channels.

2012 - 2016:

I did my undergraduate training at the University of Khartoum. I obtained a Bachelor's degree in medicine and surgery (MBBS) in 2012. In the following two years, I did my basic medical training (internship) and obtained a Master's degree in molecular medicine (M.SC.). Between 2014 and 2016, I collaborated with several Sudanese colleagues on studying the genetics of the Sudanese population in health and disease. This research was based at the Univeristy of Khartoum. I contributed to establishing the Sudanese neurogenetics group ('NeuroGeneticsSudan'), a research node at the Unisversity of Khartoum to study the genetic underpinningsd of a range of neurological conditions. The group is lead by Dr. Liena Elsayed and works on several domains, e.g.:

  • Spinocerebellar degeneration.
  • Neurodevelopmental Disorders.
  • Neuromuscular and movement disorders.
  • Epilepsy.
  • Parkinsonism.

I also worked with Prof. Muntaser Ibrahim at the Institute of Endemic Diseases on cancer and population genetics:

  • Investigating genetic predisposition to cancer in Sudanese families.
  • Understanding the population diversity and history.
